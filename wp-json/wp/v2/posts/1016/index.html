\u00c9sto me lleg\u00f3 por mail y, bueno… \u00bfes as\u00ed como nos ven los guiris?
You know you have lived in Spain when…<\/em><\/p>\n

(01)<\/strong> You think adding lemonade, fanta or even coke to red wine is perfectly acceptable. Especially at lunch time.
(02)<\/strong> You can’t get over how early bars & clubs shut back home – surely they’re shutting just as you should be going out?
(03)<\/strong> You aren’t just surprised that the plumber\/decorator has turned up on time, you’re surprised he turned up at all.
(04)<\/strong> You’ve been part of a botellon.
(05)<\/strong> You think it’s fine to comment on everyone’s appearance.
(06)<\/strong> Not giving every new acquaintance dos besos seems so rude.
(07)<\/strong> You’re shocked by people getting their legs out at the first hint of sun – surely they should wait until at least late June?
(08)<\/strong> On msn you sometimes type ‘jajaja’ instead of ‘hahaha’
(09)<\/strong> You think the precious aceite is a vital part of every meal. And don’t understand how anyone could think olive oil on toast is weird.
(10)<\/strong> You’re amazed when TV ad breaks last less than half an hour, especially right before the end of films.
(11)<\/strong> You forget to say please when asking for things – you implied it in your tone of voice, right?
(12)<\/strong> You love the phenomenon of giving ‘toques’ – but hate explaining it in English
(14)<\/strong> You don’t see sunflower seeds as a healthy snack – they’re just what all the cool kids eat.
(15)<\/strong> You know what a pijo is and how to spot one.
(16)<\/strong> Every sentence you speak contains at least one of these words: ‘bueno,’ ‘co\u00f1o,’ ‘vale,’ ‘venga,’ ‘pues nada’…
(17)<\/strong> You know what ‘resaca’ means. And you had one at least once a week when you lived in Spain.
(18)<\/strong> You know how to eat boquerones.
(19)<\/strong> A bull’s head on the wall of a bar isn’t a talking point for you, it’s just a part of the decor.
(20)<\/strong> You eat lunch after 2pm & would never even think of having your evening meal before 9.
(21)<\/strong> You know that after 2pm there’s no point in going shopping, you might as well just have a siesta until 5 when the shops re-open.
(22)<\/strong> If anyone insults your mother, they better watch out…
(23) <\/strong>You know how to change a bombona. And if you don’t, you were either lazy or lucky enough to live somewhere nice.
(24)<\/strong> You’re either a Los Serrano person or an Aqui no hay quien viva person.
(25) <\/strong>You don’t accept beer that’s anything less than ice-cold.
(26)<\/strong> The fact that all the male (or female) members of a family have the same first name doesn’t surprise you.
(27)<\/strong> The sound of mopeds in the background is the soundtrack to your life.
(28)<\/strong> You know that the mullet didn’t just happen in the 80s. It is alive and well in Spain.
(29)<\/strong> You know the differenc between cojones and cajones, tener calor and estar caliente, bacalao and bakalao…and maybe you learned the differences the hard way!
(30)<\/strong> On a Sunday morning, you have breakfast before going to bed, not after you get up.
(31)<\/strong> You don’t see anything wrong with having a couple of beers in the morning if you feel like it.
(32)<\/strong> Floors in bars are an ideal dumping ground for your colillas, servilletas etc. Why use a bin?!
(33)<\/strong> You see clapping as an art form, not just a way to express approval.
(34)<\/strong> You know ensaladilla rusa has nothing to do with Russia.
(35)<\/strong> When you burst out laughing every time you see a Mitsubishi Pajero (thanks Stuart Line for reminding me of that one!)
(36)<\/strong> You have friends named Jesus, Jose Maria, Maria Jose, Angel, maybe even Inmaculada Concepcion…<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
